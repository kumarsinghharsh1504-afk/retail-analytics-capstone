**Retail Analytics Capstone**



**Project Overview**



This project analyses over 1 million retail transaction rows to understand revenue trends, customer value, product performance and market concentration.



The project uses Python, PostgreSQL, SQL, Power BI and DAX to create a complete analytics pipeline from raw Excel data to an interactive business dashboard.



**Business Problem**



How can a retail business use transaction data to understand revenue trends, customer value, product performance and market concentration?



**Tools Used**



Python | PostgreSQL | SQL | Power BI | DAX | Excel



**Data Pipeline**



1 | Python was used to inspect, clean and validate the raw Excel data.



2 | PostgreSQL was used to store the cleaned transaction data.



3 | SQL was used to create business ready views for sales, products, customers, dates and countries.



4 | Power BI was used to build relationships, create DAX measures and design the final dashboard.



**Dashboard Pages**



1 | Executive Overview

&#x20;  Shows total revenue, total orders, units sold, customer count, average order value, monthly revenue trend, product performance, market split and customer segment revenue.



2 | Customer Analysis

&#x20;  Shows customer value by segment, customer count by segment and average recency by segment using RFM based customer grouping.



3 | Product Analysis

&#x20;  Compares top products by merchandise revenue and top products by units sold to separate revenue leaders from demand volume leaders.



4 | Project Summary

&#x20;  Summarises the business problem, tools used, key findings and recommendations.



**Key Findings**



1 | Revenue peaks in autumn, especially around October and November.



2 | The UK dominates total sales compared with international markets.



3 | Champion customers generate the largest share of customer revenue.



4 | Hibernating customers are high in count but lower in revenue contribution.



5 | Revenue leading products and volume leading products are not always the same.



6 | Administrative charges and one off bulk orders should not be treated as normal product demand.



**Business Recommendations**



1 | Prepare stock and staffing before autumn peak months.



2 | Protect Champion customers through retention activity.



3 | Reactivate At risk high value customers selectively.



4 | Use low cost campaigns for Hibernating customers because they are large in number but lower in value.



5 | Monitor both revenue leaders and volume leaders because they support different business decisions.



6 | Separate administrative charges and unusual bulk orders from normal merchandise demand.



**Limitations**



1 | The dataset is historical and does not represent current retail performance.



2 | The dataset does not include profit margin, marketing spend or customer acquisition cost.



3 | Customer segmentation is based on transaction behaviour only.



4 | Recommendations are based on sales value and customer behaviour, not operational constraints or stock availability.

It is relevant for Business Analyst, Data Analyst, Operations Analyst, Market Research Analyst and Consulting roles.
